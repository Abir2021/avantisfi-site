The problem with DeFi derivatives

Decentralised exchanges have transformed trading but still suffer from serious drawbacks. Automated market makers (AMMs) and early perpetual DEXs introduced new risks and friction that limit adoption.

Impermanent loss & MEV

AMM‑based protocols expose liquidity providers to impermanent loss and MEV bots, making it hard to provide capital reliably【764991983568758†L51-L54】.

Slippage & high fees

Limited asset coverage

Most DeFi perpetuals focus on crypto only. Access to forex, commodities and other real‑world assets is rare.

Poor risk tooling

Liquidity providers have little control over risk exposure, leading to one‑size‑fits‑all returns and vulnerability during market turbulence.

Under the hood

AvantisFi’s architecture is built for performance, security and composability. Here are some of the core building blocks:

Oracle‑based synthetic engine

Accurate price feeds underpin every trade. AvantisFi integrates reliable oracles to execute orders and enforce funding rates.

Dynamic risk tranches

LPs assign capital to different risk buckets based on volatility and open interest, similar to Uniswap v3’s price ranges but extended to time and leverage dimensions【776225662590112†L92-L109】.

Account‑abstracted wallets

Gas abstraction: fund a smart wallet with ETH for gas while trades debit USDC from your EOA. If a smart wallet isn’t enabled, you can still trade directly from your EOA with standard signatures.

Cross‑asset collateral

Hold USDC as collateral and gain exposure to multiple asset classes without moving funds across chains.

Composable APIs

Developers can build on top of AvantisFi using its SDK to integrate leveraged trading into wallets, games and prediction markets.
